the signal green rule

Signal green is the living-intelligence signal — refined from the old near-neon so it stays luxury on dark. Use it surgically only: the icon dot, active / AI states, and strategy accents. Never as fills, never on large surfaces. Restraint keeps it premium.

the gold rule

Gold has exactly two jobs: the identity color of creative & marketing, and warm atmospheric counter-light. Every composition has one counter-light — warm gold or cool gray-white, never both in the same frame. Blue coexists with either. Gold is light, never UI text or buttons.

08

lighting & atmosphere

light is emotional infrastructure

Cool base with warm accents. Subtle, atmospheric, restrained — depth without noise. Light should feel physically integrated and lived-inside, never pasted on. Darkness is foundational: without it, light loses meaning.

warm counter-light · gold
cool counter-light · gray-white

One counter-light per frame — warm gold or cool gray-white, never both. When gold isn't present, the counter-light is this gray/white glow. Blue coexists with either.

soft glow

Emotional light diffusion. Volumetric, restrained. Never radioactive.

warm light

The gold counter-light. Human warmth against the cool base.

deep depth

Shadow architecture, indigo void, cinematic contrast.

edge light

Softened diffusion, gradual falloff. Never sterile vector sharpness.

UI light should feel aerospace-grade, not gamer-tech. No neon, no glowing everything equally, no arcade HUD.

10

iconography

line-based · the blue dot

Icons are line-based, geometric, single consistent stroke, minimal, and always meaningful — never decorative. Each carries the signature blue dot (or green dot for form. strategy) as its active-signal accent. Per-department glyphs are being finalized; the system rules are locked.

stroke

Single consistent weight. Geometric construction. No filled shapes, no gradients inside icons.

the dot

The blue dot (or green dot for strategy) marks active intelligence — the icon's signal node. Restrained, precise, never oversized.

scale

Small-to-medium. Icons support hierarchy; they never dominate a composition.

meaning

Every icon earns its place. Never line-art clip art, never trend glyphs.

15

do's & don'ts

never drift from the system

do's

  • Use the dark cinematic foundation.
  • Keep blue as the master accent; department colors quarantined by context.
  • Use Satoshi (display), Manrope (body), IBM Plex Mono (metadata).
  • Keep lowercase brand behavior; the period always present.
  • Use signal green surgically inside strategy only.
  • Use one counter-light per frame — warm gold or cool gray, never both.
  • Use minimal, purposeful line icons with the blue (or green) dot.
  • Keep layouts spacious; communicate clarity and intelligence.

don'ts

  • Make blue — or any department color — the whole page.
  • Bring back near-neon green, or use green outside strategy.
  • Use Canela, Commuter Sans, uppercase FORM., or heavy weights.
  • Pair gold and gray counter-light in the same frame.
  • Use light corporate-brochure or generic SaaS-infographic styling.
  • Use cyberpunk clichés, cluttered dashboards, or loud effects.
  • Recreate the wordmark or distort the mark.
  • Drift from the current form. world.
